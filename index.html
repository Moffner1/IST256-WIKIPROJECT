Matthew Offner

Overview

Matthew Offner (born August 12, 1985) is an American painter, sculptor, and educator who describes himself as “a lifelong explorer of color, form, and human connection.” He established a multi-disciplinary practice in Brooklyn, New York, integrating his passion for storytelling into each piece. Early Life

Matthew Offner spent his childhood in Denver, Colorado, where street art and community murals first ignited his fascination with color and form. Encouraged by local youth art programs, he began sketching and painting at an early age, developing a strong desire to make creative expression accessible to everyone. This passion and sense of purpose laid the foundation for his later studies at the Rhode Island School of Design.

Education

Offner pursued formal training at the Rhode Island School of Design, focusing on painting and sculpture. During his time there, he studied under renowned mentors who introduced him to new mediums and collaborative projects. He graduated with a BFA in Fine Arts, refining the narrative-driven approach that would later shape his multi-disciplinary practice.

Career

After graduating, Offner established a multi-disciplinary studio in Brooklyn, New York, showcasing his narrative-driven artworks in local galleries. He quickly gained recognition for blending visual storytelling with public engagement, creating immersive mural projects and installations. Beyond exhibiting his work, he conducts workshops and collaborates with community organizations to promote art as a catalyst for social connection.

Personal Life

Offner currently resides in Brooklyn, New York, where he and his partner share a home that doubles as a creative workspace. An avid traveler, he often draws inspiration from international art movements to enrich his evolving style. In his downtime, he volunteers with local youth art initiatives, reflecting his ongoing commitment to fostering creative expression.
